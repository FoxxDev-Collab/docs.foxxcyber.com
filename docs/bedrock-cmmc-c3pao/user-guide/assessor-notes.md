# Assessor Notes

Using internal notes to document observations during assessment.

## Purpose of Assessor Notes

Assessor notes allow your team to:

- Document observations not included in formal findings
- Communicate between team members
- Record context for assessment decisions
- Maintain audit trail of review process

**Important:** Assessor notes are internal to your C3PAO team and are not visible to the organization being assessed.

## Types of Notes

### Control Notes

Notes attached to specific controls:
- Observations about implementation
- Questions for team discussion
- Rationale for determinations

### Evidence Notes

Notes on specific evidence items:
- Quality observations
- Verification notes
- Concerns or questions

### General Notes

Assessment-wide observations:
- Overall impressions
- Cross-cutting themes
- Team coordination

## Adding Notes

### On Controls

1. Navigate to the control
2. Click the **Notes** tab
3. Enter your observation
4. Click **Add Note**

![Screenshot: Adding control note](/img/screenshots/c3pao/add-control-note.png)

### On Evidence

1. Open the evidence item
2. Click **Add Note**
3. Enter your observation
4. Save the note

### General Assessment Notes

1. Go to the assessment overview
2. Select **Notes**
3. Add your observation
4. Categorize as appropriate

## Note Visibility

| Note Type | Visible To |
|-----------|------------|
| Control Notes | Assessment team only |
| Evidence Notes | Assessment team only |
| General Notes | Assessment team only |
| Findings | Organization and team |

## Note Best Practices

### Be Specific

Include relevant details:
- What you observed
- Why it matters
- Any follow-up needed

### Be Professional

Notes may be reviewed for quality assurance:
- Use professional language
- Focus on facts
- Avoid personal opinions unrelated to assessment

### Be Timely

Document observations when they occur:
- Don't rely on memory
- Note context that may be forgotten
- Include timestamps for reference

## Team Collaboration

### Flagging for Discussion

Use notes to flag items for team discussion:
- Uncertain determinations
- Conflicting evidence
- Complex scenarios

### Mentioning Team Members

Tag team members for attention:
- Use @mentions to notify
- Assign follow-up actions
- Track resolution

### Resolution Tracking

Mark notes as resolved when addressed:
- Keeps active items visible
- Provides audit trail
- Supports quality assurance

## Note Templates

Common note structures:

### Observation Note
```
Observation: [What you saw]
Context: [Relevant background]
Impact: [Why it matters]
Follow-up: [Any needed action]
```

### Question Note
```
Question: [What needs clarification]
Context: [Why you're asking]
Requested from: [Team member or customer]
Status: [Open/Resolved]
```

### Determination Rationale
```
Control: [Control ID]
Determination: [Met/Not Met/N/A]
Key Evidence: [What supported decision]
Rationale: [Why this determination]
```

## Exporting Notes

Assessment notes can be exported for:

- Quality assurance review
- Team debriefs
- Record keeping
- Lessons learned

Navigate to **Notes > Export** to download.
