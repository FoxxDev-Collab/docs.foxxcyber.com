# Accepting Assessment Requests

Learn how to review and respond to incoming assessment requests.

## Incoming Requests

When an organization requests an assessment from your C3PAO, you'll receive a notification and the request will appear in your engagements list with **Requested** status.

![Screenshot: New assessment request](/img/screenshots/c3pao/new-request.png)

## Reviewing Request Details

Before accepting, review the request details:

### Organization Information

- Company name and contact details
- Industry and size
- Previous assessment history (if any)

### Scope Information

- CMMC level requested
- Systems and assets in scope
- Estimated complexity

### Package Overview

- Current SSP status
- Control implementation progress
- Evidence availability

### Requested Timeline

- Preferred assessment dates
- Certification deadline requirements
- Scheduling flexibility

## Responding to Requests

### Accepting a Request

1. Click **Accept Request**
2. Review and confirm the engagement terms
3. Assign a lead assessor (or accept yourself)
4. Confirm the proposed timeline or suggest alternatives
5. Submit your acceptance

![Screenshot: Accept request dialog](/img/screenshots/c3pao/accept-request.png)

### Declining a Request

If you cannot accept the engagement:

1. Click **Decline Request**
2. Select a reason:
   - Scheduling conflict
   - Conflict of interest
   - Outside service area
   - Capacity limitations
   - Other (with explanation)
3. Optionally provide additional context
4. Submit your response

The organization will be notified and can request assessment from another C3PAO.

## After Accepting

Once accepted, the engagement moves to **Accepted** status:

- The organization is notified of your acceptance
- You can begin reviewing preliminary documentation
- Coordinate with the customer on assessment logistics
- Assign additional team members as needed

## Best Practices

- **Respond promptly** - Organizations are often on tight timelines
- **Review thoroughly** - Ensure you can meet the requested scope and timeline
- **Communicate clearly** - Set expectations early about the assessment process
- **Check for conflicts** - Verify no conflicts of interest exist
